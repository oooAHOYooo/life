## PLATFORM FOR CAMPING GEAR

### What It Is
- Elevated wooden deck (4-8 ft × 8-12 ft)
- Keeps gear dry, organized, off ground
- Doubles as workspace, seating area
- No permit needed (not a structure, it's site prep)

### Build It Yourself

**Materials** (~$200-300):
```
Pressure-treated lumber (2×6, 2×4):   $80-120
Concrete blocks (16):                  $30-50
Bolts, screws, fasteners:             $30-40
Tarps/waterproofing:                  $40-60
Tools you probably have:               $0
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TOTAL:                                 $180-270
```

**Design** (Simple):
```
Level ground → place concrete blocks as posts
→ lay beams (2×6 lumber) → screw down joists (2×4) → add plywood decking

No footings needed (it's temporary, removable)
No permit needed (under 200 sqft, no permanent posts)
```

**Time**: 1-2 days solo, 4-6 hours with a friend

**Why**: 
- Keeps sleeping bag, tools, supplies dry
- Elevation prevents pest/water damage
- Doubles as deck for sitting/working
- Easy to remove if you build cottage later

## YURT: THE PERFECT TEMPORARY SHELTER

### Why a Yurt?

**PERFECT for your situation**:
- ✅ Portable (not a "permanent structure")
- ✅ Legal to camp in during construction
- ✅ Beautiful aesthetic
- ✅ Weather-resistant (better than tent)
- ✅ Can be removed when cottage built
- ✅ Used by builders/campers nationwide

### How to Handle Permits

**Technically**: A yurt is "temporary camping equipment" not a building
- Can live in yurt during construction phase
- Just like RV
- Building permit typically allows it

**If asked by inspector**:
- "Living in yurt while building cottage"
- "Temporary shelter during owner-builder phase"
- Both legally sound

**Reality**: On raw rural land in Catskills, no one checks closely

### Yurt Permitting (Honest Answer)

**What Happens**:
1. Get building permit for cottage (shows active construction)
2. Permit language usually says: "RV, camper, or temporary shelter allowed during construction"
3. Yurt fits that category
4. Inspector might visit, sees yurt, notes "owner camping on-site"
5. No violation

**If They're Strict About It**:
- Rename it "Temporary Camping Shelter" in permit
- Most jurisdictions have 12-18 month construction window during which camping is legal
- Yurt is explicitly allowed in most owner-builder permits

**Reality**: Every builder in America camps on-site during construction. This is normal, expected, legal.

## STONE PATIO/HARDSCAPE: OUTDOOR VIBES

### Why This Is Great

✅ **No permit needed** (landscaping, not building)
✅ **No footings** (sits on ground)
✅ **Beautiful aesthetic** (gathering space)
✅ **DIY-friendly** (physical work, no skill)
✅ **Can use salvage stone** (free/cheap)
✅ **Scalable** (add to it over time)
✅ **Can incorporate utilities later** (sink, fire pit, shelter)

### Stone Patio Design

**Simple Pattern**:
```
Clear area (level ground)
  ↓
Landscape fabric base (prevents weeds)
  ↓
Crushed stone/gravel (2 inches deep)
  ↓
Lay stones in pattern (mortared or dry-laid)
  ↓
Finished: outdoor dining/gathering space
```

**No footings required** = Not considered a "structure" = No permit

### Cost & Materials

#### Option A: Salvage Stone (CHEAPEST) — $0-500

**Where to find**:
- Craigslist ("free stone" posts constantly)
- Facebook Marketplace
- Demolition sites (ask permission, often free)
- Old walls, driveways people are removing
- You pay hauling only

**Cost**:
```
Free stone:                            $0
Gravel base (crushed stone):           $50-150 (buy one load)
Landscape fabric:                      $30-50
Mortar (if using):                     $50-100
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TOTAL:                                 $130-300
```

**Size**: Can do 12×16 ft patio with free stone

#### Option B: Bought Stone (NICE) — $400-1,000

**Stone types**:
```
Flagstone (regional, pretty):          $3-6/sq ft
Slate (beautiful):                     $5-8/sq ft
Bluestone (Catskills favorite):        $4-7/sq ft
Recycled brick (local):                $2-4/sq ft

For 200 sq ft patio:                   $400-1,200
```

**What you'd buy**:
```
Stone (200 sq ft):                     $400-800
Gravel base:                           $100-200
Landscape fabric:                      $30
Mortar (optional):                     $50-100
Sand base:                             $30-50
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
TOTAL:                                 $600-1,200
```

### DIY Stone Laying (Easy)

**Dry-Laid (No Mortar)** - Easiest:
1. Clear/level ground
2. Lay landscape fabric (prevents weeds)
3. Spread 2" gravel base
4. Lay stones in pattern
5. Fill gaps with gravel/sand
6. Done

**Time**: 2-3 days for 200 sqft with a friend
**Skill**: None needed (it's just moving rocks)
**Maintenance**: Rake gravel back in every year or two

**Mortared (Nicer Look)**:
1. Same as above but...
2. Use mortar between stones (like regular masonry)
3. Cleaner look
4. More permanent
5. Takes longer (mortar cures 48 hrs between sections)

**Time**: 4-5 days with curing time
**Cost**: +$100-200 for mortar
**Result**: Professional looking, lasts 20+ years
