## CAN YOU LEGALLY CAMP THERE WHILE BUILDING?

### YES - With These Caveats:

**Short Answer**: Yes, you can live in an RV/camper/tent on raw land during construction in NY.

**Real Requirements**:

1. ✅ **Living in RV/Camper While Building**
   - Allowed during active construction
   - Must have "contractor on-site" or "owner building"
   - Typically allowed 12-18 months during construction phase
   - Need construction permit (usually includes RV clause)

2. ✅ **Camping in Tent**
   - Trickier - technically "temporary shelter"
   - But on your own land, very hard to enforce
   - Many people do it without issue
   - If questioned: "Living in RV during construction"

3. ❌ **Permanent House Trailer (Without Utilities)**
   - Harder to permit if not tied to active construction
   - But once you have construction permit, easy

4. ⚠️ **Duration**
   - Construction permit typically valid 12-18 months
   - Can be renewed if building continues
   - Reality: No one checks closely on raw land in Catskills

5. ✅ **What You ACTUALLY Need**
   - Building permit for cottage (includes RV allowance)
   - Septic if you want permanent plumbing (eventually)
   - Electric optional (can use generator)
   - Water can be trucked initially

## WATER & SEPTIC: DO YOU NEED THEM TO CAMP?

### Short-Term Camping (6-12 months)
- **Water**: Use trucked water ($50-100/delivery) or buy bottled
- **Septic**: Composting toilet in RV/outhouse, or portable camping toilet
- **Electric**: Generator ($500-2,000) or solar panels ($3-5K)
- **Cost**: ~$0-500 for camping setup

### Long-Term (While Building Cottage)
- **Well**: Add when ready to build ($5-9K) — can do year 2-3
- **Septic**: Add when building house ($4-10K) — required before occupancy
- **Electric**: NYSEG line can be added later ($0-2K if within free allowance)

### Advantage of Waiting on Utilities
- ✅ Camp first, see how you like the land
- ✅ Figure out cottage placement before drilling well
- ✅ Find volunteers/materials first, spread costs over time
- ✅ Zero pressure to complete anything quickly

**Why it works**:

**What You Tell Inspectors**:

- Legal in NY with proper permits

**Timeline**:
