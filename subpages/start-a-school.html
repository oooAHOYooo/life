Start a School

School / development studio — ideas & vision

Inspired by

  • Nintendo
  • Starting a school / development studio

Model: School + Studio

  • Students at the end can join a collective or form their own LLC. If they want the “advance route,” they can start their own LLC and put their products to market.
  • Internally, the studio also has products for market — kids would be the playtesters.
  • The school/studio would make Video Games + Films.
  • Schedule: During the day, teach kids; mornings + nights make games/film — and “all the time sell coffee.”

Kids & programming

  • Luekens (and others)
  • Open to kids 11–15
  • $100/month membership for “bASICS” (basics tier)
  • Classes: 4pm–5pm
  • Building exits: 5:15pm

Todo — launch & run

  • Find space (classroom + studio + coffee area)
  • Insurance (liability, property)
  • Curriculum: path from basics → ship a product → form LLC
  • Get relationships with schools (referrals, after-school)
  • Set up bASICS tier: $100/mo, 4–5pm, exit 5:15pm
  • Coffee setup (equipment, supplier) — “all the time sell coffee”
  • First cohort: recruit Luekens + others (ages 11–15)
  • Studio products pipeline (games/film) so kids can playtest
  • Document “advance route”: how students form LLC and launch

Ways to make money (easy + other)

Low-friction ideas and broader revenue so the school can stay afloat and keep the focus on students building real products and forming LLCs.

Easy ways to increase monthly rev

  • Teach classes / workshops — One-off or short series (e.g. “Intro to game dev,” “Make a short film”). Price per session or per series. Parents and adults pay; kids already there can upsell.
  • Parent or adult classes — “Code with your kid” or “Game design basics.” Fills seats and builds community; often higher willingness to pay.
  • Merch / small products — T-shirts, stickers, or simple “student game” downloads. Margin on each sale; builds brand and a bit of recurring rev.
  • Coffee upsells — Monthly “coffee membership” or punch cards. Locks in regulars and smooths revenue.
  • Charge a bit more across the board — Slightly higher bASICS, coffee prices, class fees, and higher-tier tuition. Same volume, more rev per unit.
  • More higher-tier students — Advance route or premium tracks at $250–300/mo. Same teaching time, higher rev per seat.
